# Goals Plan (GP)

## Definition
The Goals Plan (GP) formalizes the commitments of an elected government by translating political promises into structured objectives, quantified goals, and concrete initiatives to be delivered during the administrative term.  
It is a governance instrument designed to increase transparency, accountability, and social control over government action.

**Source:**  
São Paulo Municipality (2008) – Organic Law Amendment No. 30/2008  
➡️ https://www.prefeitura.sp.gov.br

---

## Purpose
The primary purpose of the Goals Plan is to ensure that the elected government’s agenda is transformed into measurable, monitorable, and publicly accountable actions throughout the mandate.  
It connects political commitments to implementation mechanisms, enabling systematic monitoring and citizen oversight.

## Minimum Structural Components

- **Thematic Axes** — Broad thematic areas that structure governmental priorities and organize commitments  
  (UN-Habitat, 2020)  
  ➡️ /references/bibliographic-references.md#un-habitat-2020

- **Strategic Objectives** — Expected impacts and outcomes associated with each thematic axis  
  (OECD, 2019)  
  ➡️ /references/bibliographic-references.md#oecd-2019

- **Goals** — Quantified and time-bound commitments to be achieved during the mandate  
  (São Paulo Municipality, 2008)  
  ➡️ https://www.prefeitura.sp.gov.br

- **Initiatives** — Projects, programs, and concrete actions linked to each goal  
  (UNDP, 2016)  
  ➡️ /references/bibliographic-references.md#undp-2016

- **Indicators** — Metrics used to monitor progress and assess goal achievement  
  (Inter-American Development Bank, 2020)  
  ➡️ /references/bibliographic-references.md#inter-american-development-bank-2020
