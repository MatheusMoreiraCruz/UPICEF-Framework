# Budget Guidelines Law (LDO)

## Definition
The Budget Guidelines Law (LDO) defines the goals and priorities of public administration for the following fiscal year and guides the preparation of the Annual Budget Law (LOA), as established by Article 165 of the Brazilian Federal Constitution.  
Source: [CF/1988](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#cf1988)

The LDO is further detailed by the Fiscal Responsibility Law, which establishes fiscal rules and planning discipline for public administration.  
Source: [Fiscal Responsibility Law – LC 101/2000](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#lrf2000)

## Purpose
To connect the Multi-Year Plan (PPA) with the Annual Budget Law (LOA), adjusting priorities, fiscal targets, and budgetary parameters on an annual basis.  
Source: [Fiscal Responsibility Law – LC 101/2000](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#lrf2000)

## Minimum Structural Components

- **Goals** — Fiscal and policy outcome parameters for the fiscal year  
  ([Fiscal Responsibility Law – LC 101/2000](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#lrf2000))

- **Priorities** — Actions and policy areas considered central for the fiscal year  
  ([CF/1988](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#cf1988))
