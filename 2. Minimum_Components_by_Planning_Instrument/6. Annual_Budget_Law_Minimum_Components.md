# Annual Budget Law (LOA)

## Definition
The Annual Budget Law (LOA) is the legal instrument that estimates public revenues and fixes public expenditures for a single fiscal year, as provided by Article 165 of the Brazilian Federal Constitution and regulated by Law No. 4.320/1964.

Sources:  
- [Brazilian Constitution (1988)](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#brazilian-constitution-1988)  
- [Law No. 4.320 (1964)](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#law-no-4320-1964)

## Purpose
To detail the annual execution of public policies, ensuring consistency with the Multi-Year Plan (PPA) and the Budget Guidelines Law (LDO).

Source:  
[Brazilian Constitution (1988)](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#brazilian-constitution-1988)

## Minimum Structural Components

- **Budget** — Revenue estimates and expenditure authorizations for the fiscal year  
  ([Brazilian Constitution, 1988](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#brazilian-constitution-1988);  
  [Law No. 4.320, 1964](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#law-no-4320-1964))

- **Alignment Annex with the LDO** — Demonstration of compatibility between the LOA and the priorities established in the LDO  
  ([Fiscal Responsibility Law, 2000](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#fiscal-responsibility-law-2000))
