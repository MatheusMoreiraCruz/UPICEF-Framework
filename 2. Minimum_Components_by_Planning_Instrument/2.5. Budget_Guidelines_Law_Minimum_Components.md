# Budget Guidelines Law (LDO)

## Definition
The Budget Guidelines Law (LDO) defines the goals and priorities of public administration for the following fiscal year and guides the preparation of the Annual Budget Law (LOA), as established by Article 165 of the Brazilian Federal Constitution.

Source:  
[Brazilian Constitution (1988)](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#brazilian-constitution-1988)

It is further regulated by the Fiscal Responsibility Law, which establishes fiscal targets and parameters for public budgeting.

Source:  
[Fiscal Responsibility Law (2000)](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#fiscal-responsibility-law-2000)

## Purpose
To connect the Multi-Year Plan (PPA) to the Annual Budget Law (LOA) by annually adjusting priorities and fiscal parameters.

Source:  
[Fiscal Responsibility Law (2000)](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#fiscal-responsibility-law-2000)

## Minimum Structural Components

- **Targets** — Fiscal and policy result parameters for the fiscal year  
  ([Fiscal Responsibility Law, 2000](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#fiscal-responsibility-law-2000))

- **Priorities** — Actions and policy areas considered central for the fiscal year  
  ([Brazilian Constitution, 1988](https://github.com/MatheusMoreiraCruz/UPICEF-Framework/blob/main/references/bibliographic-references.md#brazilian-constitution-1988))
